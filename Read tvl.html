What is TVL Strand?

TVL stands for "Technical-Vocational-Livelihood." It is a specialized track or program in the senior high school level that focuses on developing technical and vocational skills. The TVL strand is designed to provide students with practical knowledge and hands-on experience in various trades and vocations. The curriculum includes subjects related to specific technical fields such as agriculture, automotive technology, electronics, information and communication technology, hospitality, and other vocational areas. The goal is to prepare students for employment in specific industries or to pursue further education and training in technical fields.

Why TVL?

Opting for the TVL (Technical-Vocational-Livelihood) strand offers opportunities in technical and vocational fields, emphasizing practical skills and hands-on training. Common courses include automotive technology, culinary arts, electrical installation and maintenance, and hospitality management. TVL programs aim to prepare students for immediate entry into the workforce. Graduates can pursue careers as technicians, chefs, electricians, and in various skilled trades. Alternatively, they may choose to further their education by enrolling in technical-vocational courses or apprenticeships, enhancing their expertise and advancing in their chosen fields.
